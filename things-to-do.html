Things to Do

The Buffalo National River, located in Arkansas, is the first river in the United States to be designated a national river.
It offers abundant wildlife, scenic views, and excellent opportunities for exploring the outdoors.


Hiking

Crazy hikes are avaliable for the adrenaline seeking adventurer, as well as gentler hikes for those who wish to take life a little easier. Whether it be one-day excursions or multiple-day adventures that stretch the length of the river, the possibilities are endless and right at the tip of your hiking boots.


Wildlife Watching

Did you know that the Buffalo National River is home to Arkansas' only elk heard? And right here in the heart of Buffalo River Country is where you can find large groups of our beautiful Searcy County Elk. The best practices for viewing Searcy County Elk is to come late in the evening or early in the morning.


Stargazing

The Buffalo National River gained the title of an International Dark Sky Park in 2019. That is because the Buffalo River has limited lighting that preserve the beauty and resource that is a dark night sky for a premium stargazing experience.


Historic Sites

Step back in time by visiting the many preserved homesteads, mills, and cemeteries throughout the Buffalo River region. Tyler Bend and Rush Historic District offer self-guided tours that showcase the area's rich history, from pioneer life to the zinc mining boom of the late 1800s. It's a great way to connect with the human stories of the Ozarks.


Rock Climbing

The towering limestone bluffs along the Buffalo National River are a climber’s paradise. Whether you're a seasoned rock climber or just starting out, areas like Sam’s Throne and Horseshoe Canyon Ranch offer thrilling routes with stunning views.
